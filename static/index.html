What the numbers mean

Watchlist columns

Price
The stock price retrieved by this symbol's most recent scan (Yahoo data, ~15 minutes delayed) — not a live tick. The colored arrow is the day change versus the previous session's close: ▲ up, falling shows ▼ in red.
IV/HV
At-the-money implied volatility divided by 20-day historical volatility. Near 1.0, options are priced in line with how the stock has actually been moving. Above ~1.25 (highlighted), options are pricing in more movement than the stock has delivered — a catalyst is expected, or premium is rich. Well below 1.0, optionality is cheap relative to realized movement. The small Nd is the horizon: the days to the nearest expiry this IV is read from. It varies by ticker (a few days for daily-listing ETFs, weeks for single names), so compare IV/HV across names with the horizons in mind.
IV rank
Where the current ATM IV sits in its own trailing range, 0–100 (the bar fills to the rank). High (amber, ≥60) = IV is rich versus this name's own history — options expensive, the move is more anticipated than usual. Low (green, ≤30) = IV cheap. Unlike IV/HV, this is comparable across names. The window grows toward a year as history accrues; expand a row for the IV sparkline and percentile. Glitchy outlier readings are filtered so one bad print can't distort the range.
P/C
Today's put volume ÷ call volume across the scanned expirations. Balanced names sit roughly 0.5–1.2. High (≥2) means heavy put activity — hedging or bearish bets; low (≤0.4) means heavy call activity. The ratio is meaningless on a few opening prints, so alerts on it wait 45 minutes into the session and require minimum volume.
⚡
Number of signals this symbol fired in the last 24 hours.

Detail metrics (click a row)

ATM IV
Average implied volatility of the call and put closest to the stock price in the nearest scanned expiry. It's the market's annualized estimate of future movement: 47% IV implies roughly a ±3% expected daily move (annual ÷ √252). IV rises when traders pay up for options — usually ahead of expected news.
HV 20d
Annualized volatility the stock has actually realized over the last 20 trading days, from daily closes. The backward-looking counterpart to IV: comparing the two tells you whether options are expensive or cheap relative to how the stock really moves.
Call vol / Put vol
Total contracts traded today across the scanned expirations. Volume resets daily and builds as the session goes on.
Peak γ strike
The strike with the heaviest gamma × open-interest concentration — the chain's center of gravity. Dealer hedging is most active there, and near expiry it tends to act as a magnet: price gets sticky around big-gamma strikes ("pinning"). When this strike jumps to a new level between scans, positioning is shifting.
Skew (p−c)
IV of a ~5% out-of-the-money put minus a ~5% out-of-the-money call. A few points positive is normal — investors persistently overpay for downside protection. Rising skew = crash protection getting bid. Negative (inverted) skew = calls trading richer than puts, the footprint of an upside chase. The change matters more than the level.
Net GEX /1%
Estimated dollars of stock that options dealers must trade to stay hedged per 1% move in the underlying. Positive: dealers sell rallies and buy dips — their hedging dampens volatility and favors range-bound, pinning behavior. Negative: their hedging amplifies moves — faster trends, squeezier tape. Caveat: the sign assumes dealers are long calls and short puts; in names dominated by speculative call buying, the true sign can flip.

Signals

IV premium
ATM IV is at least 1.25× the 20-day historical vol — options are pricing in a much bigger move than the stock has been making. Either the market expects a catalyst, or premium is rich enough to interest sellers.
IV spike
ATM IV rose ≥10% versus the average of this session's recent scans — someone is bidding up options right now. Most interesting when the stock price isn't moving.
Unusual volume
A contract is on pace to trade at least 2× its open interest today (volume is projected to full-day pace, so morning and afternoon are judged fairly). Volume far above OI means new positions being opened, not old ones closing.
Put/call ratio
Today's flow is heavily one-sided: puts ≥2× calls (fear or hedging) or calls ≥2.5× puts (upside speculation).
Gamma build
Net gamma exposure grew ≥25% since the previous scan, on the same side it was already on. Building positive (stabilizing): dealer hedging leans harder against moves — expect tighter ranges and pinning. Building negative (destabilizing): hedging amplifies moves — expect bigger swings. The message states which one it is and names the strikes driving the exposure. Only fires when GEX is big enough to matter (default ≥ $5M per 1% move) — below that, dealer hedging is too small to move the tape.
Gamma flip
Net gamma exposure changed sign since the previous scan — a regime change, the most consequential gamma event. Flipping negative (red): dealer hedging switches from cushioning moves to chasing them; faster, trendier, squeezier price action. Flipping positive: the tape should calm down and get sticky around big strikes. Subject to the same materiality floor as gamma build, and names the driver strikes so the source can be checked on the chain.
Gamma pin
The peak gamma strike sits within 2% of the stock price — the setup for pinning, where hedging flows hold price near that strike into expiry. Expect stickiness, not a breakout, unless news hits.
Setup read
Synthesizes two things you'd otherwise combine in your head: where IV sits in its own range (cheap vs rich) and which way the current flow leans (call-heavy / put-heavy / inverted skew / short fuel). Cheap vol + bullish flow = inexpensive optionality on an upside setup; rich vol + a lean = the move is well-priced, so defined-risk spreads beat naked longs. A way to frame the structure of the opportunity — not a recommendation, and defined-risk only.
Confluence
Three or more different signal types fired on the same name within a few hours. Any single detector can be noise; several independent ones agreeing is the strongest pattern this app produces — it's what flags names like "inverted skew + heavy call volume + unusual contract activity" as one story instead of three alerts. Names with a confluence in the last 24h get a 🔥 in the watchlist.
Squeeze setup
The preconditions for a short/gamma squeeze lining up: heavy short interest (≥10% of float — the fuel, always required) plus at least three of: call-dominated flow, fresh near-dated OTM call buying, inverted skew, and price rising with IV rising. If it runs, short covering and dealer hedging push the same direction. Caution: when net GEX reads positive on one of these, the dealer-positioning assumption is likely backwards — speculators bought those calls, so dealers are short them. Short-interest data is FINRA bi-monthly, up to ~2 weeks stale.
Vol compression
The "coiled spring": 10-day realized vol has collapsed well below the 20-day while options aren't pricing an expansion. Quiet, tight ranges tend to resolve violently — in either direction. This is a setup flag, not a directional call.
Daily wrap
An end-of-day summary card (4:15 PM ET) with one row per name: day move, ATM IV with the day's change in vol points, and the most significant signal of the session. The "what stuck" footer lists unresolved confluences and conditions still elevated at the close — your checklist for tomorrow's open. Quiet names are listed as quiet: silence is information too.
Skew shift
Skew moved ≥4 vol points versus this session's baseline. Toward puts: downside protection being bought. Toward calls: upside being chased.

Conventions & caveats

Severity colors
blue = informational, yellow = notable, red = strongest readings. Each symbol+signal pair has a 45-minute cooldown so the feed isn't spammed by the same condition every scan.
Catalyst tags
Signals note the earnings calendar: "earnings in Nd" explains an expected IV ramp, while "no obvious catalyst" marks vol being bid with nothing scheduled — historically the more interesting pattern. No tag means the earnings date couldn't be determined.
Negative numbers
Shown in parentheses, finance-style: (6.2%) means −6.2%.
Data & timing
Quotes are ~15 minutes delayed and illiquid strikes can be stale. Scheduled scans run 9:30–16:00 ET on weekdays only; comparison signals baseline against the current session, so the first scans each morning are intentionally quiet. None of this is financial advice — signals flag anomalies worth researching, not trades.
